Education Is My Passion - HemiNotes Is My Dream






“They ask me what I do and who I do it for" - 2 Chainz


Written by Eric Nambo, HemiNotes founder and SFA Junior


One of my earliest memories of my older sister was when she would come home from elementary school, pull out her chalkboard easel, and show me everything she had learned that day.

I learned my multiplication tables before I could even say “multiplication tables”.

From an early age, I began to love learning about the world around me. My mom would read me tidbits about the world out of an encyclopedia as a bedtime story. My sister taught me math and vocabulary. My older brother taught me about military history sparking my love for research.

When I came of age, public school began to take the role as my primary means of education.

And that’s when I lost my love for learning.

I sat in a desk, got talked at and took tests that the state said I had to pass.

In high school, I learned how to skate by with minimal effort and minimum grades. I graduated 2011 with a measly 3.23 GPA, bottom 75th percentile of my graduating class.

When I arrived at college, I didn’t know what to expect. I was hoping that a change in the quality of educators would rekindle my passion for learning. And while the educators are indeed more engaging, what I discovered took me by surprise:

The quality of students and their excitement to share what they learned truly inspired me.

I would bump into friends on campus that would leave their class hyped about a world event that they wanted to talk about. I often found myself chatting with a classmate after leaving stats, talking about a particular equation that “got weird when you applied infinity” (as my professor liked to say.)

I felt immersed in learning, like I did when I was a kid. And my first two semester GPA’s, 3.78 and 4.0, showed it.

It inspired me to want to be a teacher. But after a stint as a tutor at the university, I realized traditional teaching wasn’t as fulfilling for me.

It took me a couple of years of working for the distance education department (read: online classes/learning) and working with visually impaired students to help me figure out how I could use technology to inspire excitement. I even picked up a few awards along the way: SFASU Student Employee of the Year 2014, and Desire2EXCEL, an international award by D2L “given to individuals, groups, or organizations that have displayed exemplary ingenuity and creativity in empowering students to achieve their academic goals.”

I’ve taken every ounce of my experience, both personal and professional, and combined them with Piero’s, to give you HemiNotes, my vision and dream of providing a learning tool to help students inspire and be inspired by others’ academic success.

I want HemiNotes and the services we provide to give you that feeling of meaningful and personal immersion that my family and fellow students give me. I want you to be excited to share what you learned in class and I want the students who struggle to be excited to learn from you.

Together, lets make learning more personal by making it more collaborative. Our generation has prided itself on using technology to create innovative solutions to help each other. And while HemiNotes has a long way to go, we hope that we can inspire you to be apart of our effort.

With a month still left in this semester, already over 60 students have pledged to donate their notes to our cause.
