Why We Started HemiNotes






Written by Piero Pretto, SFA alumni.



I was 14 years old when I told them I had a learning disability.

My teachers looked at each other in disbelief.

“I try so hard in school,” I said. “I study really hard for my exams and read my textbook and stuff but nothing sticks. I can’t learn.”

I was sitting in a vacant classroom surrounded by teachers and the school principal. This intervention was designed to help students who were struggling at the charter school. Tears poured out my face as shame enveloped me.

“You’re a smart kid, Piero,” my English teacher said. “You’re doing better in my class.”

I recently transferred to the charter school because I couldn’t meet the “rigorous” requirements of a public school system. I was skimming above a C average at my new alma mater.

The rest of the intervention went as anticipated. My teachers told me I would do better and I left the room with the same hopelessness I carried throughout my entire childhood.

What I didn’t anticipate was that I would one day graduate from a public high school Magna Cum Laude. Or that I would attend SFASU on an academic scholarship. Or that I would make a 4.0 GPA my first semester there, write a blog about it - “How To Make a 4.0 at SFA” - and attract over 2,500 student viewers in 78 hours.

I was like most others students who struggled in the classroom. I thought I was dumb.

So what changed?

My sophomore year of highschool, I was lucky enough to share a seat on the school bus with the future salutatorian of my high school. After he showed me his notes and gave me some pointers on how to study I came to realization that I wasn’t a complete moron. I just didn’t know how to study.

I asked the smartest kids in my class how they prepared for the exams I failed. I learn the importance of tracking your study time. But more importantly, I saw how students who knew nothing about a subject learned to grasp it over time. All this was accomplished by borrowing the minds of my peers versus that of a teacher who had mastered the material a long time ago.

We started HemiNotes because it has the power to save thousands of students from the shame I carried for so long. We offer handwritten notes from start to finish so students can learn as the semester progresses. We use Box to not only display those notes but to allow students to leave comments and exchange ideas with their peers. But most importantly, you get to borrow the mind of someone who had the confidence to donate to our cause. That’s pretty exciting.

We have a long way to go at HemiNotes to make our services bigger and better. But we belive every student can make great grades. We belive in the power of peer-to-peer learning. And we love our generation’s willingness to give freely - despite the stereotypes assigned to us by previous generations.
