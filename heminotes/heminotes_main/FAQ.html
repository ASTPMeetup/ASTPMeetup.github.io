Frequently Asked Questions



1) Is sharing and viewing other student's notes legal? Does HemiNotes offer test banks or other material that could help me with assignments and exams?

HemiNotes will not acquire or distribute exams, essays, or material that could be considered plagiarism or under the ownership of Stephen F. Austin State University or its faculty in compliance with the Texas Education Code. HemiNotes will solely acquire and distribute material created by students of Stephen F. Austin State University that has not been used to directly determine an academic grade. HemiNotes is in no way affiliated or endorsed by Stephen F. Austin State University or any other university.

Furthermore, Notes-n-Quotes, a business serving students at Texas A&M University, has been offering similar services to HemiNotes for 23 years. These conditions give us reason to believe that our website is both ethical and lawful in its practices.

2) How are notes viewed and displayed on this website? Can they be downloaded?

As of August 21, 2014, notes will be available exclusively online for viewing using Box enterprise software. Notes are not available for download at this time but this is subject to change.

3) Can I sell my notes to HemiNotes?

No. Donations keep our website free for students to use. We hope this will serve to increase the number of notes available on our site. Our mission is to have a set of course notes for every class offered at SFA.

4) Where is the comments tab? I want to see what other students are saying about each class!

The comments tab will show if you click on the quote bubble icon at the top right of every set of notes.
